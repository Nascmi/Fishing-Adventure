# Monetization

Monetization is not part of the current roadmap. If introduced later, it must support the game without changing the quality or pace of free play.

## Never Allowed

- Advertisements
- Energy systems or paid wait-skips
- Loot boxes or randomized purchases
- Paid gameplay power
- Artificial inconvenience sold back as a solution
- Expiring offers designed to create pressure

## Potentially Acceptable

- Cosmetic rods and boats
- Cabin decorations
- Clearly described cosmetic bundles
- A straightforward supporter pack

Prices, purchase restoration, parental considerations, platform requirements, and offline behavior must be designed deliberately before commerce is implemented.

## Prepared Real-Money Product Catalog

The project contains dormant planning metadata for nine possible non-consumable products. None are imported by the live app, visible to players, configured on a platform, or available for purchase.

- Workshop Cabin — tentative $1.99
- Lakeside Cottage — tentative $1.99
- Coastal Shack — tentative $1.99
- Workshop Details — tentative $0.99
- Cottage Comforts — tentative $0.99
- Coastal Memories — tentative $0.99
- Cabin Palette Collection — tentative $0.99
- Cabin Collection bundle — tentative $4.99
- Lifetime Charter License — tentative $4.99

Prices are planning targets only; platform tiers, taxes, and regional pricing may change them. Every product is permanent and non-consumable. The three cabin products buy complete authored rooms. Detail and palette packs may only fill compatible cosmetic slots. The bundle grants the same cabin entitlements as the three individual cabin products and no exclusive gameplay content.

The Lifetime Charter License is a convenience purchase, not an unlock or fishing advantage. A player must unlock a destination, pay for its first charter, and complete all three days normally. The license then waives repeat charter fees at that completed destination. It never changes catch rates, rewards, trophy odds, or trip length.

Before any product becomes visible, implementation must provide authoritative platform entitlements, purchase restoration, clear owned states, offline behavior, parental and age-rating review, regional product availability, and failure-safe handling. No product may grant fish, coins, rods, keepsakes, paintings, trophies, Master Angler frames, progress, or improved fishing odds.

## Earned-Coin Trading Post

The in-game Trading Post is a separate department of the existing shop using only coins earned through fishing. Its live cabin catalog follows a clear 25,000, 50,000, and 75,000-coin progression, providing visible goals for players who have completed their equipment progression while preserving the 100,000 Coin Club plaque as the first six-figure prestige purchase. Cabin listings disclose their authored customization-hook counts. Twelve additional rugs, display frames, timber finishes, small cabin objects, and the prestige plaque retain planned IDs and prices and appear as disabled previews until their customization slots work in-game.

The initial stock does not rotate, and owned items remain permanent. A future rotation may only be introduced with a predictable return schedule. The Trading Post never sells fish, catch odds, equipment power, charter progress, keepsakes, earned location art, trophies, or real-money currency. Included cosmetics and accomplishment rewards remain distinct. Prices require continued playtesting against late-game income.
